Higantes Festival

Where

Originated at Angono, Rizal

When

Celebrated every November 22

Description

Higantes Festival is a colorful celebration held in Angono, Rizal, Philippines, honoring the town's patron saint, Saint Clement. The festival is known for its giant papier-mâché effigies, called "higantes," which can reach up to 12 feet tall. These effigies are paraded through the streets, accompanied by music, dancing, and revelry. The festival is a lively and vibrant showcase of Angono's artistic heritage and community spirit, drawing both locals and tourists alike to witness the spectacle.














"The Feast of San Clemente,"

HIGHLIGHTS

The highlights of the Higantes Festival include the vibrant and colorful parade of giant papier-mâché effigies, known as "higantes," which are often creatively designed and adorned with elaborate costumes. The festive atmosphere is enhanced by music, dance performances, and street food stalls, creating a lively and immersive experience for attendees. The festival also celebrates the cultural heritage and traditions of Angono, Rizal, showcasing the artistic talent and craftsmanship of the local community. Overall, it's a visually stunning and culturally rich event that captivates both locals and visitors alike.
