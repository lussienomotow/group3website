Bangus Festival

Where

Dagupan City

When

Celebrated every April 30

Description

The Bangus Festival is a lively celebration in the Philippines, particularly in Dagupan City, Pangasinan. It honors the humble milkfish, or "bangus," which is a significant part of the local economy. The festival features street dancing, culinary competitions, and various activities centered around this beloved fish. It's a vibrant showcase of local culture and traditions.














"Silver Jewel"

HIGHLIGHTS

The Bangus Festival, typically held in April, spans several days of festivities that blend tradition, entertainment, and culinary delight. One of the festival’s main attractions is the Bangusan Street Party, where locals and visitors alike gather for a lively street dancing competition. Dancers don colorful costumes representing the bangus, and the streets come alive with pulsating music, intricate choreography, and a contagious spirit of merriment.At the heart of the festival is the Bangusine Festival, a cooking contest that showcases the versatility of the bangus as a culinary ingredient. Chefs and cooks compete to create innovative and mouthwatering bangus-based dishes, from traditional recipes to fusion creations that demonstrate the fish’s adaptability to various cooking techniques and flavors.
