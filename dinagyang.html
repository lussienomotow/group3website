Dinagyang Festival

Where

Originated at Iloilo City

When

Celebrated every Fourth Sunday of January

Description

The Dinagyang Festival is an exhilarating and world-renowned celebration that takes place in Iloilo City, Philippines. It showcases the rich cultural heritage and unwavering spirit of the Ilonggo people.The festival’s origins can be traced back to the 1960s when a replica of the Santo Niño (Child Jesus) from Cebu was brought to Iloilo. Inspired by the Sinulog Festival in Cebu, the Dinagyang Festival was born as a way to honor the Santo Niño and promote Iloilo’s cultural identity.














"Iloilo Ati-Atihan"

HIGHLIGHTS

The highlight of the festival is the Ati-Atihan competition, where participating tribes showcase their awe-inspiring choreography and synchronized movements. The performers, with their faces painted in traditional Ati warrior motifs, captivate the audience with their energetic dances and rhythmic drumbeats. Beyond the lively performances, the Dinagyang Festival also offers a range of cultural activities and events. Visitors can explore the festival’s bazaar, which features local crafts, delicacies, and souvenirs. Traditional music and dance performances, art exhibits, and food festivals provide a deeper immersion into the rich heritage of Iloilo.
