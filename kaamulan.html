Kaamulan Festival

Where

Originated at Malaybalay,Bukidnon

When

Celebrated every last week of February, ends second week of April

Description

The Kaamulan Festival is a vibrant and culturally-rich celebration held annually in the province of Bukidnon, Philippines. Rooted in the traditions and customs of the indigenous tribes of Mindanao, it showcases the unique heritage and diversity of the region. It is considered one of the most significant and colorful festivals in the region. “Kaamulan” is a Binukid term, which means “gathering” or “festival.”














"Gathering Of The 7 Tribes Of Bukidnon"

HIGHLIGHTS

The highlight of the Kaamulan Festival is the grand street dancing competition. During this event, participants from different indigenous tribes in Bukidnon showcase their cultural heritage through vibrant costumes, rhythmic dances, and traditional music.The street dancing competition is a spectacle of colors, energy, and skill. Participants, adorned in elaborate traditional attire, perform synchronized dances accompanied by the beat of indigenous musical instruments. The performances depict various aspects of the tribes’ cultural practices, stories, and traditions.
