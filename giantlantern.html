Giant Lantern Festival

Where

Originated at San Fernando, Pampanga

When

Celebrated every mid of December - 1st of January

Description

The Giant Lantern Festival is an annual event that lights up San Fernando city for local and visitors from mid-December until New Year’s Day. Due to its popularity, this city has been nicknamed the “Christmas Capital of the Philippines”.The predecessor of this festival was a religious activity. Lanterns created in each barrio from bamboo and other locally available materials, were brought around each barrio during the nine-day novena before Christmas.

HIGHLIGHTS

The highlights of the Giant Lantern Festival include the stunning and intricate lantern designs, the vibrant colors, the lively atmosphere with music and performances, and the sense of community as people come together to celebrate this cultural event. Each lantern is a work of art, showcasing the creativity and craftsmanship of the local artisans. The festival is a feast for the senses, offering visitors a unique and unforgettable experience.The most exciting part of the Giant Lantern Festival is probably the moment when all the lanterns light up together, creating a dazzling display of colors and patterns. It's like witnessing a magical transformation as darkness is illuminated by vibrant light.
