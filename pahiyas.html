Pahiyas Festival

Where

Originated at Lucban,Quezon

When

held every 15th of May

Description

The Pahiyas Festival is a colorful celebration held annually in Lucban, Quezon, Philippines. It honors San Isidro Labrador, the patron saint of farmers, and marks the harvest season. Houses are adorned with vibrant decorations made from local produce such as rice, fruits, and vegetables, creating a stunning display of creativity and gratitude. The festival also features street parades, traditional music, and local delicacies, making it a vibrant cultural experience for both locals and tourists.














"Harvest Festivals to Honor San Isidro Labrador"

HIGHLIGHTS

The most exciting part of the Pahiyas Festival is arguably the moment when locals unveil their intricately decorated houses to the public. As visitors stroll through the streets of Lucban, they are greeted by a kaleidoscope of colors and creativity, with each house competing to outshine the others in terms of beauty and ingenuity. Witnessing the unveiling of these stunning decorations, crafted from local produce with meticulous attention to detail, is a truly awe-inspiring experience that captures the essence of the festival's spirit and showcases the talent and creativity of the community.
