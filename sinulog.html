Sinulog Festival

Where

Originated at Cebu City

When

Celebrated every Third Sunday of January

Description

Sinulog is held in celebration of one of the country’s most famous historic relics: the Santo Niño de Cebú. This was the statue of the baby Jesus that was handed to the Rajah Humabon of Cebu by the Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan in 1521. Sinulog comes from the Cebuano word “sulog” which means “like water current movement” which depicts the forward-backward step of Sinulog dance.














"Queen of all Festivals"

HIGHLIGHTS

The Sinulog Festival's main highlight is the Sinulog Grand Parade, which is a nearly day-long event that features a foot parade, floats, and street dancing. Participants wear vibrant costumes and dance to the beat of drums and chants in honor of the Santo Niño, the city's patron saint. The parade also includes a rhythmic dance that follows the steps of the Cebuano folk dance, "maglalatik".
