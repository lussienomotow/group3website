Pintados-Kasadyaan Festival

Where

Tacloban City, Leyte, Philippines

When

Celebrated every June 29

Description

The Pintados Festival, also known as the Pintados-Kasadyaan Festival, originated in Tacloban City, Leyte, Philippines in the 1980s. The festival is based on the body-painting traditions of the ancient "pintados" warriors, who were native people in the Visayas with permanent tattoos on their bodies and faces as a sign of social status. The word "pintados" means "tattooed ones" in Spanish














"Festival of festivals of Leyte"

HIGHLIGHTS

This is a fascinating display of the culture and traditions of the people of Leyte. The festival also features a variety of art and cultural activities, such as painting and sculpture workshops, pottery demonstrations, and a parade of traditional costumes.
