Moriones Festival

Where

Originated at Marinduque

When

Celebrated every Holy Week

Description

This is a week-long celebration participated in by male penitents in costumes who parade around town. On Good Friday, they reenact the story of Longinus, a centurion, blind in one eye, who speared Jesus’ side. Christ’s blood fell on his blind eye, miraculously restoring it, leading to his conversion.














" Re-enactment of the Biblical Story of Saint Longinus"

HIGHLIGHTS

The most exciting part of the Moriones Festival is usually the reenactment of the story of Longinus, the Roman centurion who pierced the side of Jesus with his spear during the Crucifixion. It's a visually stunning spectacle with participants wearing colorful costumes and intricately designed masks, creating a vibrant and lively atmosphere.
